Registration Rules

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)

1.       In what languages can the project be presented?

●      Official languages of NAVARACHANAA are English and Hindi. Projects should be prepared and presented only in these languages.

2.       Who all can participate?

●      Anyone who is pursuing course from Engineering and Science Colleges.

3.       What are the project categories?

●       There are 6 categories which are as follows:

                                                       I.            Smart City

                                                    II.            Digital India

                                                 III.            Make in India

                                                 IV.            Swachh Bharat Abhiyan

                                                    V.            Skill India

                                                 VI.            General Category;This category is sub categorized into following

A.    Computer Science and Information Technology

B.     Electronics And Communication/Electronics and Instrumentation /Electrical and Electronics.

C.     Civil Engineering

D.    Mechanical and Automobile Engineering

E.     Textile Engineering

                        Please note that A,B,C,D and E ,have separate prizes.

4.       How and where to register a project for NAVARACHANAA?

●      Each project can be represented by a team of maximum of 4 students and minimum of 2 students.

●      Students may represent one or more teams. The team name should be different from the other.

●      Steps for registration:

1.      Visit the link : www.navarachanaa.svvv.edu.in/registration.php

2.      Click Register now.

3.      Fill the Registration Form (Participants are responsible for the legitimacy of the information they enter during registration (false information) as these may lead to rejection of your process application. Our organizing committee will not be responsible for their mistakes. In case a change is required, please email us before the end of the registration date at navarachanaa@svvv.edu.in . All other queries be directed at this email address as well)

4.      After Registration you will get a confirmation mail, if you don’t receive mail within 24 hours of registration please register again or contact NAVARACHANAA team.

5.      If your idea is shortlisted, you will receive a confirmation e-mail from the committee.

6.      The short-listed teams will have to pay the fees within 72 hours of receiving confirmation mail of their selection for the next round, payment details are mentioned below.

7.      The selected teams shall report on 29th April for second round (Project Presentation).

8.      The teams which qualify for the third round will present themselves before the jury with project demonstration.

5.       What do I do if my project is qualified for the Round II?

▪       Participants must bring along all the hardware and software required for their project-pen drives, cables, equipments required for your project.

▪       Each team is expected to hand out some form of documentation to help the jury to identify their project. The hand outs should not contain repetitions or prolongations. It should be concise and focused on the subject, and not having omissions. Participants can use any of the materials mentioned above for this purpose.

▪       Participants should prepare for a presentation (presentation is a generic term here and does not necessarily refer to a PowerPoint presentation) for the jury. Each team will be given approximately 15 min. Time for your presentation and including questions from the jury.

▪       After that on the basis of presentation, your project will be judged and if your project qualifies for final round then you need to demonstrate your project.

6.       Accommodation

o      We do have an accommodation facility at SVITS Guest House and Hostels for participants (subject to availability of accommodation space) on payment basis.

o     We do not provide any transportation if participants decide to stay in  another hotel.

7.       Presenting your project to jury

o     You will be notified to which jury you need to deliver your presentation and in which slot.

o     Jury rooms are open to everybody. You can monitor the judging of any project at any time you wish. We believe this sets the transparency and the fairness of judging process.

o     A simple presentation (15 minutes) should not contain more than 10 slides and should cover the following points

▪      Introducing yourself

▪      Describing the project-

1.      Literature Survey

2.      Problem Formulation

3.      Project Description

4.      Technology Used

5.      Application

6.      Reference

a) Books

b) Websites

c) Research paper

 

  NOTE:

After your presentation there will be “Question and Answer by Jury” round.

            All rules are subject to change without any prior notice by organization committee.
